## Objective

\<Describe here the problem this design addresses. Objective sections should be
solution neutral, i.e. they should not specify in any way the mechanism used to
solve the problem. Once more for emphasis: don't mention solutions in the
objective section. This section should be brief, no more than a couple of
sentences.\>

## Background

\<Use this section to fill in any gaps in understanding that need to be bridged
before your peers can properly understand the design. Common topics to cover
include descriptions of previous attempts to solve the problem, prior art and related
problems, and the state of existing systems relevant to problem solution.

This section rarely needs to run longer than two or three paragraphs.\>

## Requirements and Scope

\<Describes the requirements that a design must meet to be satisfactory, and

and a bulleted list of non-goals is usually sufficient for this section.\>
